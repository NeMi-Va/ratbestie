Living With Pet Rats

Cage:

Get an appropriately sized cage for your pets!
The usual recommendation is 2 cubic feet per rat but if you can buy a bigger cage, please do it!
So, the more rats you have, the larger the cage should be. If you have two rats it should be up to 4 cubic feet at a minimum.
Avoid cedar or pine bedding, as the aromatic oils can harm their respiratory system. Choose paper-based or felt bedding instead.

Letting the rats out of their cage:

It is crucial to let your rats out of their cage no matter the cage size.
Let rats out at least 2 times a day 1 hour at a time.
By letting your rats roam in a controlled environment, you bond with them as well as make their lives a lot more fun!

Rat proofing the roaming space:

Close all the holes through which your fur baby can escape to explore the unknown territories by using hard-to-chew items.
Don’t use cloths as rats will pull them out nor e.g., cardboard as rats will chew threw it.
Hide anything and everything you don’t want to be bitten or chewed.
Even if the rat is not trying to eat your stuff their curious nature will often make them try everything by chewing it a little.
Before letting the rats out always remember to close all the needed doors/cabinets.

Spaces to hide:

Because rats are prey animals, they need spaces to hide to feel comfortable.
Provide cardboard boxes or old sheets where the rats can burrow.

Spaces to climb:

Rats love to climb, hence try to allow them to climb where they want.
If the climbing spot is not safe close it off by using pet fences or an improvised fence from e.g., big container lid.
If possible, get them a cat tree to climb onto.
